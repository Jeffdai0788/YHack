What to Expect
What is a hackathon?
So you’ve decided to come to one without knowing what one is. A hackathon is a short-timeframe software and hardware creation event. In this context, to “hack” means to create or to build. At YHack, you’ll meet fellow hackers, learn new technologies, and build a project you can take home. We'll have free food, swag, workshops, mentorship, prizes, games, and more. Come with or without a team or an idea, and we'll give you the space to build something awesome.
I’ve never been to a hackathon and I’m nervous.
Excellent! You’ll find yourself just at home.
Do I need to know how to code?
No programming experience is necessary. We will have plenty of resources to help you during the event. We also offer workshops for a broad range of skill levels.

Packing List 


Here’s what to pack for the weekend:
* Government-issued ID and university ID
   * You will need to show both forms of ID to be let into the venue
   * These IDs are NOT optional. They are incredibly incredibly mandatory. You WILL be turned away at the venue if you cannot present both forms of ID.
* Computer(s) and accessories (mouse, keyboard, etc.)
* Chargers (phone, laptop, etc.)
* Any hardware you might want to hack with. Don’t bring anything oversized, dangerous, or illegal (don’t bring a whole CNC machine)
* Sleeping things (sleeping bag, pillow, eye mask)
* A change of clothes, ideally a warm set (New Haven weather is unpredictable!)
* Hygiene stuff
   * Toothbrush/toothpaste
   * Deodorant (please)
* Legal medication if needed

RULES


In order to be eligible to win a prize, your team and project must follow all of the below rules and guidelines:
1. All members of your team must have checked-in to YHack (needless to say, they must have all been accepted to the hackathon)
2. At least one member of your team must be physically present to demo your project to judges on Sunday (check schedule for specific time)
3. All hacking and building must have taken place during hacking hours (11:00am on Saturday, March 28 - 11:00am on Sunday, March 29).
   1. Note: brainstorming is allowed (and encouraged) before the hackathon, but all coding and physical building must take place during hackathon hours
   2. We will verify that winning work is completed by only members of the team and completed within the correct timeframe. We WILL check your GitHub repo.
4. Your team cannot contain more than 4 people.
5. This is an MLH event. By participating in this event, you agree to abide by the MLH Code of Conduct.
